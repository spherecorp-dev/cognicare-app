## PART 1: Email Strategy (The Big Picture)

### Voice & Personality

**From:** WAGGO (but written like a PERSON, not a brand)

**Tone:** Friendly expert friend who happens to know everything about pet nutrition. Direct. Warm but not corporate. Occasional sarcasm when talking about bad pet food industry practices. NEVER sounds like a marketing email.

**Personality archetype:** "The friend who's really into pet nutrition and sends you texts about it" — except those texts are actually useful and you look forward to them.

**Adapted from my methodology:**
- SaaS vertical = less crude, more helpful
- Pet parents = emotional audience, respect that
- But NEVER boring. NEVER corporate. NEVER "Dear valued subscriber."
- Plain text always. No graphics. No fancy HTML.
- Sell in every email. No exceptions. No apologies.

**Why daily (eventually)?**

Because when you email daily, you train your list to EXPECT you. To OPEN you. To TRUST you.

And trust = money.

But for a new SaaS like WAGGO, start with weekly after onboarding. Move to daily once you have enough stories, tips, and angles to sustain it. Target: daily by month 3.
